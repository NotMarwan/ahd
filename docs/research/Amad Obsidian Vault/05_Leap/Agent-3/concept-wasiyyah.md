# وصية · Wasiyyah — leave it right, while you're alive
> *Wasiyyah (وصية)* = the bequest. The deed almost every Saudi owes and almost none has done.

## A. One-liner
**A living Islamic estate that computes your exact faraid shares against your *real* connected net worth, notarizes your ≤⅓ wasiyyah through Nafath, and keeps itself current as your life changes — so the most precise, most neglected religious duty becomes one calm, finished thing.**

## B. Problem
Faraid (the divinely-fixed inheritance shares) is precise, mathematically intricate, and a religious obligation — yet almost no Saudi has a current, valid plan; families are left to confusion and disputes at the worst moment. Existing tools are **free static calculators** (HalalWallet, Al-Wirasat) — a number on a page, disconnected from your actual wealth, not legally executed, never updated. **Why now:** OB can value your real net worth; **Nafath** can e-notarize a wasiyyah (the AWQAF digital-certificate precedent proves e-deeds work); ALLaM explains the shares in plain Arabic.

## C. Solution
1. **Connect** (OB) → your real, live net worth (accounts, investments, property entered once).
2. **Compute** → exact faraid distribution across your heirs, explained per-share in Arabic ("your daughter receives ⅛ because…").
3. **Bequeath** → draft + **Nafath-notarize** the discretionary **≤⅓ wasiyyah** (a cause, a person, or **an [[concept-athar|Athar]] waqf** — leave a perpetual charity in your will).
4. **Stay current** → it auto-flags life events (marriage, a birth, a new asset) and prompts an update; includes debts + digital assets + a recorded message to heirs.
- **Only-now/here:** a *connected, notarized, self-updating* estate — not a calculator. Only an Islamic bank can fuse the faraid engine + real net worth + Nafath + a halal bequest.
