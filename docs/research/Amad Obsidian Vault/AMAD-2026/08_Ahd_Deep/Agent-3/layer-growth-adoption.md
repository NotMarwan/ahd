# Ahd — Behavioral, Adoption & Growth (Deep Layer)

> The flagship adoption thesis for a bank-witnessed rail for money between people. This is not a restatement of the concept — it is the three-layers-deeper closure of every growth hole the red-team and teardown exposed. Arabic-first terminology, English gloss inline. Every load-bearing number is cited or marked as a designed assumption.

---

## 1. Audit findings (compressed — full version in `audit-growth.md`)

The growth layer of the champion concept rests on five bare assertions: "viral by construction," "guest-Nafath join," "universal + daily," "framing flips the cultural objection," "lender issues, borrower accepts." None has a mechanism, an incentive, or a number. The four findings that can lose the round:

1. **Virality is structurally a tax, not an engine** — the second person is the hardest invitee in fintech (you are asking them to *admit a debt and be bound to it*), yet the concept treats their join as automatic. k-factor is never computed.
2. **The cultural wound is named in 11 words and never sutured** — there is no copy, tone, or sequence that performs the reframe from "you doubt me" to "we protect each other."
3. **"Daily" is false** — interpersonal lending is episodic; there is no recurring hook, so no D30 and no bank relationship.
4. **The hero feature (Muqassa) is the least-reachable thing in the product** — it needs an entire circle live before it can fire.

## 2. Gap register (compressed — full table in `gaps-growth.md`)

12 gaps, 4 Critical (k-factor/G1, invitee-incentive/G2, cultural-reframe/G3, recurring-hook/G4), 5 High, rest Med. The rest of this document closes them in order, with mechanism, copy, algorithm, numbers, and edge-cases.

## 3. Deep resolutions (the bulk)

### 3.1 — The first-agreement funnel, step by step (closes G1, G10)

The funnel is not "open app → invite → done." It is a precise 7-state machine, instrumented at every transition. Inviter = **الدائن** (lender/initiator). Invitee = **المدين** (the one who owes / the counterparty).

```
[F0] Inviter opens Ahd (existing Alinma customer — warm install via Alinma app deep-link)
        │  reason: a real pending favour ("I just lent Sara 5,000")
        ▼
[F1] Inviter drafts the عهد  — picks kind (قرض حسن / shared cost / deferred / gift)
        │  ALLaM drafts plain-Arabic fair terms; riba-clean check runs
        ▼
[F2] Inviter SIGNS first (Nafath)  ← critical inversion: the LENDER commits first,
        │  so the invite arrives as "Noura has already given her word," not a demand
        ▼
[F3] Invite delivered to counterparty (WhatsApp / SMS deep-link — NOT app-gated)
        │  the link opens a WEB preview of the covenant; no install required to READ it
        ▼
[F4] Counterparty reads the covenant in plain Arabic (the reframe copy, §3.3)
        │  decision point: Agree / Propose change / Decline
        ▼
[F5] Counterparty identity step  → guest-Nafath (§3.6).  KYC-light, no Alinma account needed
        ▼
[F6] Counterparty SIGNS (Nafath) → witnessed record issued; both see «عهدٌ موثّق» (a witnessed covenant)
        ▼
[F7] Auto-settle armed (sarie) · dhimmah tracked · both now retained users
```

**The load-bearing inversion (F2 before F3):** in the original concept the borrower is invited to *create* the obligation. Here **the lender signs first and gives, then invites.** The counterparty is not asked "will you admit you owe me?" — they are shown "I have already honoured my word to you; confirm you received it." This single re-ordering converts the invite from an accusation into a gift-receipt. It is the difference between virality and offence.

**Instrumented drop-off model (designed assumptions, labelled):**

| Transition | Conversion (modelled) | Why this number |
|---|---|---|
| F3→F4 (link opened) | 85% | Deep-link to a known person about real money you're owed/owe → high open. KSA WhatsApp penetration ~ universal. |
| F4→F5 (decides to proceed) | 70% | The reframe copy (§3.3) carries this; declines route to §4. |
| F5→F6 (completes guest-Nafath) | 80% | Nafath is in 75% of Saudi adult pockets already [Nafath ~17.2M downloads, ~75% of adults — staff4all/DGA, 2025]. |
| **F3→F6 overall** | **~48%** | 0.85 × 0.70 × 0.80. Conservative; see k-factor §3.5. |

### 3.2 — The two-sided incentive: why person 1 invites and why person 2 joins (closes G2)

**Inviter (الدائن) incentive — three layers:**
1. *Protection of the asset* — the money is on the line; a witnessed record is the only thing standing between "lent" and "lost." ≈30% of borrowers never fully repay and ≈50% never write it down (*US proxy — KSA figure pending*; the KSA-grounded anchor is the **ubiquity of the سند لأمر** and **43M+ Najiz e-services in H1-2024**). Ahd is the writing-down, free.
2. *Protection of the relationship* — the data they keep is proof the trust was honoured, not doubted (the reframe, §3.3).
3. *Auto-settlement* — they never have to send the awkward "where's my money?" message again; the rail does the asking (§3.9), so the friendship is never the collector.

**Invitee (المدين) incentive — the part the concept omitted entirely:**
1. *Dignity / clearance of ذِمّة* — the strongest motivator in the design. A witnessed schedule means the obligation ends *visibly and finally*; «ذمّة محفوظة» on completion is closure they cannot get from a verbal IOU that lingers forever. The product gives the borrower **the right to be considered settled** — something they cannot get any other way.
2. *Fairness guarantee* — the record is riba-clean and penalty-free by construction; signing Ahd is proof they will **never** be charged interest or a surprise penalty. For a Muslim borrower this is a protection, not a trap.
3. *Reciprocity unlock* — once on Ahd, the invitee can themselves be a lender/initiator next time, and inherits the same protection. The borrower today is the lender next month.
4. *No-install to read* — they evaluate the whole thing before committing anything (F4 is web, no app).

### 3.3 — The cultural reframe as an actual copy + tone system (closes G3, part of G8)

This is the deepest hole and gets the most ink. The objection: *asking someone you love to e-sign an IOU reads as سوء الظن (suspicion), غدر (betrayal of trust), or a public accusation.* The fix is not a slogan — it is a **copy doctrine** plus exact on-screen Arabic at each emotional beat.

**Copy doctrine — five rules:**
1. **Never name the borrower as a risk.** No "protect yourself," no "in case they don't pay." The frame is always *mutual* and *both-protecting*.
2. **Lead with the deed, not the debt.** "You gave" / "أعطيتَ" before "they owe."
3. **Anchor in faith, gently, never preachy.** One Quranic touch (2:282), never a sermon, never a fatwa.
4. **The bank is the witness, never the judge.** Language is *نُوثّق* (we document) / *شاهد* (witness), never *نُحاسب* (we hold to account).
5. **Dignity verbs only.** *حِفظ* (safeguarding), *عهد* (covenant/honour), *ذمّة محفوظة* (obligation honoured). Never *دين متأخر* (late debt), *مديونية* (indebtedness).

**On-screen copy at each beat (Arabic + gloss):**

| Beat | On-screen Arabic | Gloss / why it lands |
|---|---|---|
| Inviter starts | «وثّقوا المعروف بينكما — لا لأنّكما تشكّان، بل لأنّ كلمتكما تستحق أن تُحفظ.» | "Document the kindness between you — not because you doubt, but because your word deserves to be kept." Names the suspicion and dissolves it in one line. |
| The 2:282 touch | «﴿إِذَا تَدَايَنتُم بِدَيْنٍ … فَاكْتُبُوهُ﴾ — توثيقُ الدَّيْن سُنّةٌ تحفظ المودّة.» | Quran says write it down; documentation safeguards affection. Faith reframes it as obedience + love, not distrust. |
| Invite to counterparty (the make-or-break screen) | «نورة وفّت بوعدها وأعطتك ٥٠٠٠ ريال — قرضًا حسنًا بلا أي فائدة. أكّد استلامك، ولك أن تُسدّد بالراحة المتفق عليها. لا فوائد، لا غرامات — كلمةٌ محفوظة بينكما.» | "Noura honoured her word and gave you 5,000 — qard hassan, zero interest. Confirm receipt; repay at the agreed ease. No interest, no penalties — a word kept between you." The deed first, the protection (no riba/no penalty) explicit, the obligation last and softened. |
| Completion | «ذمّةٌ محفوظة. وفّيتما بعهدكما — وبقيت المودّة.» | "An obligation honoured. You both kept your covenant — and the affection remained." The product's soul: the relationship *survived intact*, the explicit promise of the whole thing. |

**Tone spec:** warm, brief, second-person plural where possible (كما = "you both") to keep it mutual; one faith touch max; the bank's voice is a quiet trustee, never a creditor. Default to optional — the borrower can always reply privately rather than in-app.

### 3.4 — The retention engine: earning the third open (closes G4, G10)

Lending is episodic, so retention cannot rely on lending frequency. The engine has three independent return-drivers so the third open is over-determined:

**Driver 1 — Recurring & scheduled covenants (converts episodic → periodic).** Many real interpersonal obligations are *recurring*: monthly rent to a flatmate, a domestic worker's salary, a standing family contribution, repaying a loan in installments. Ahd's recurring-agreement object turns one sign-up into months of touchpoints: each settlement event is a return-trigger («دفعة هذا الشهر محفوظة»). This alone moves the median user from ~4 events/yr to **monthly** contact.

**Driver 2 — The covenant ledger (دفتر العهود) as a living surface.** Every active covenant the user is party to — as lender or borrower — lives in one ledger that updates on each settlement, reminder, or completion. The user returns not to *create* but to *watch progress* and feel obligations resolve. Each «ذمّة محفوظة» is a dignity dividend that pulls them back.

**Driver 3 — Kept-word reputation (سُمعة الوفاء), private by default.** A personal, *self-facing* streak of covenants honoured (see §3.8 for the dignity-safe design). It is a returning ritual ("my word is kept") not a credit score and never a public shame board.

**Retention model (designed cohorts, labelled assumptions):**

| Metric | Target | Mechanism that earns it |
|---|---|---|
| D1 | 55% | First covenant is live; settlement armed; user checks it resolved/armed. |
| D7 | 35% | First reminder/settlement event lands; counterparty interaction. |
| D30 | 22% | First recurring settlement OR a *second* covenant (reciprocity, §3.2) OR Muqassa prompt. Without Driver 1 this would be ~8% — the recurring hook roughly triples D30. |
| Events/user/yr | 4 → **18** | Recurring covenants + reciprocal role-switching convert episodic to periodic. |

### 3.5 — k-factor model with numbers (closes G1)

k = i × c, where i = invites sent per activated user, c = conversion per invite to an activated user.

- Every covenant requires ≥1 counterparty → **i ≥ 1 structurally**. Empirically, a new lender often has 2+ pending favours to formalise, and Muqassa circles pull in 3–4. Conservative blended **i = 1.4**.
- c = F3→F6 ≈ **0.48** (§3.1), times an activation factor (counterparty creates *their own* covenant later) ≈ 0.55 → effective new-initiator conversion **c ≈ 0.26**.
- **k = 1.4 × 0.26 ≈ 0.36.**

Honest read: **k < 1**, so Ahd is *not* self-sustaining purely organically — the original "viral by construction" claim is **false as stated, and this model proves it.** That honesty is itself defensible. Two amplifiers close the gap:

1. **Onboarding-friction wedge (Musaned domestic-worker salaries, §3.7):** the mandate is **one-sided** — employers must pay wages into a wallet; the worker is a passive recipient (Mada-withdrawable), and a closed Wage-Protection channel does not admit a third-party interpersonal qard. So it guarantees mass employer+worker wallet/KYC **presence** by 1 Jan 2026 (lowering onboarding friction for an adjacent product) but does **not** by itself create a two-sided interpersonal rail or fix k<1 — that still needs a separate GTM. It seeds *presence*, not *virality* (ledger C10).
2. **Circle seeding (§3.10):** Muqassa's i jumps to 3–4 inside a circle, pushing local k > 1 within dense groups (flatmates, families) even if global k < 1.

The defensible claim is therefore: *"Ahd is not blindly viral; it is **regulation-seeded and circle-amplified**, with organic k≈0.36 supplemented to k>1 inside its beachhead segments."* That is a far stronger and more honest growth story than "viral by construction."

### 3.6 — Guest-onboarding state machine for non-customers (closes G5)

The unanswered question: can a non-Alinma customer complete a binding e-signature inside an Alinma record? Designed answer — a **two-tier identity model**:

```
Counterparty taps invite link (no app, no account)
      │
      ▼
 Tier-0 "Witness identity"  ──► Nafath auth ONLY (no bank account opened)
      │   Nafath returns a verified national-ID/Iqama assertion.
      │   This is sufficient to e-SIGN under the Electronic Transactions Law
      │   (signature = identity-bound intent), WITHOUT a banking relationship.
      │   The signed record is the legal artefact; no money account needed to WITNESS.
      ▼
 If counterparty must RECEIVE/SEND money via sarie:
      │
      ▼
 Tier-1 conversion  ──► open a light Alinma wallet/account (KYC already 90% done
                         by Nafath) — offered, not forced. Many will, because the
                         money has to land somewhere; non-customers can also receive
                         to their EXISTING bank via sarie IBAN (no conversion needed).
```

**Key insight that de-risks G5:** *witnessing* (signing) requires only Nafath identity, not an account — so the guest-sign is legally and technically lighter than feared. *Settlement* can route to the counterparty's existing IBAN over sarie (the national instant rail reaches all banks), so **conversion to Alinma is an upsell, not a gate.** The funnel never hits a hard KYC wall. (Honest flag: exact Nafath-for-non-Alinma-service scope needs partner confirmation — §7.)

"Everyone" is replaced by a **sequenced beachhead ladder** — densest, most-forced, most-reachable first:

The obligation is **employer-side only** (the worker is a passive recipient; a closed Wage-Protection wallet, not an interpersonal rail). | A recurring قرض-حسن-clean *salary covenant*: employer commits monthly via sarie, worker's receipt is witnessed, both have a tamper-evident record — exactly what the mandate demands, with dignity. **This is the presence on-ramp that lowers onboarding friction — it does not by itself fix k<1 (ledger C10).** |
| **2** | **Flatmate rent & bill splits** | Dense circles, monthly recurrence, Muqassa-native. | Recurring shared-cost covenant; Muqassa nets the month's tangled bills in one tap. Drives Driver-1 retention + circle k>1. |

### 3.8 — Reputation as private dignity, not public score (closes G7)

The fork the concept never resolved: visible score = shaming engine (PDPL + relationship toxic); invisible = inert. Resolution — **سُمعة الوفاء (kept-word reputation) is self-facing and consent-gated:**
- **Default: private.** Only you see your own "covenants honoured" count. It is a personal dignity ritual, never shown to counterparties. No shame surface.
- **Opt-in, scoped reveal:** a user may *choose* to share a "trust badge" (e.g. «وفّى بـ ١٢ عهدًا») with a *specific* prospective counterparty, with explicit PDPL consent, for a *specific* covenant — like a reference, not a public rating.
- **Never a credit input.** Explicitly walled from underwriting (the concept's own promise — Ahd never underwrites). This keeps it out of SAMA credit-scoring scope and PDPL automated-decision concerns.

This makes reputation a *return-driver* (Driver 3) without ever becoming a weapon.

### 3.9 — Reminder choreography (closes G8)

The most dangerous surface in the app. Design rules:
1. **The rail asks, never the lender.** Reminders come from Ahd as a neutral trustee, so the friendship is never the debt-collector. The lender does **not** get a "we nudged them" receipt by default (it would make them the watcher).
2. **Tone = dignity, not dunning.** «تذكير لطيف: دفعة عهدك مع نورة تستحق خلال ٣ أيام — متى ما تيسّر.» ("Gentle reminder: your covenant payment with Noura is due in 3 days — whenever it eases for you.") Note "whenever it eases" — qard-hassan ethic, no pressure.
3. **Auto-settle removes most reminders entirely** — if sarie auto-debit is armed, no human ever has to ask. The reminder is a fallback, not the default.
4. **Escalation is mutual and faith-anchored, never punitive:** if a payment lapses, the prompt offers *reschedule together* (إعادة جدولة) framed as ease (يُسر), never penalty. Only on genuine refusal does the record's evidentiary role (MoJ/Taradhi) quietly exist — never threatened in-app.

### 3.10 — Circle-seeding sequence for Muqassa (closes G9)

Muqassa needs a live circle. Bootstrapping path so the hero feature becomes reachable:
1. **Seed via rung-1/2 cohorts:** flatmates and Musaned households already arrive as *groups*, not individuals — circles pre-exist.
2. **Circle invite as a unit:** an organiser can invite a whole circle at once (group trip, flat, family) — one action creates N pending covenants.
3. **Partial-circle Muqassa:** the netting algorithm runs on whoever is on Ahd; non-members appear as "pending" edges that resolve when they join → each net-out *demonstrates* value and pulls the holdouts in (a visible "you'd save 3 transfers if Khalid joined").

**Netting algorithm (already built in prototype — greedy min-transfer):** given net balances per person, repeatedly match the largest debtor to the largest creditor. Complexity **O(n log n)** per round (sort), at most n−1 transfers total → overall **O(n² log n)** worst case, trivial for real circles (n < 20). The prototype reduces 9 tangled IOUs → 2 transfers, deterministically. The growth contribution: each reduction is a *shareable wow* that recruits the missing edges.

## 4. Edge cases & failure modes (each with handling)

| Edge case | Risk | Handling |
|---|---|---|
| Borrower **refuses to sign** | Funnel dead; lender feels exposed | Lender's first-sign still produces a *one-sided declared record* (a dated, Nafath-signed statement of the lender's act) — weaker evidentially but not nothing; app offers a softer "acknowledge by reply" path before full Nafath. Never pressures. |
| Relationship **sours mid-covenant** | Reminders become weapons | Rail-sends-only (§3.9); either party can pause reminders; dispute path is quiet escalation to MoJ/Taradhi, never an in-app threat. |
| Counterparty is a **non-customer with no Nafath** (rare; expat without Absher) | Can't guest-sign | Fallback to Tier-0 lite identity + manual KYC; or settle to existing IBAN with a lighter (less evidentially strong) acknowledgment. Flagged honestly. |
| **Partial circle** for Muqassa | Hero feature can't fully net | Partial-circle netting (§3.10) + "invite the missing edge" prompt. Value still demonstrated. |
| Borrower **can't pay on schedule** | Default + relationship damage | Reschedule-together (يُسر) flow; qard-hassan ethic = ease, never penalty. No riba ever accrues (riba-clean by construction). |
| **Reminder fatigue / perceived nagging** | Uninstall | Frequency cap, opt-out, auto-settle removes the need; tone audited (§3.3). |
| **Asymmetric literacy/comfort** (elderly lender, young borrower) | One side can't navigate | Web-read preview (no install) + plain Arabic + voice-readable terms; delegate-assist mode. |

## 6. Adoption implication — why a Saudi trusts, uses, and returns

A Saudi trusts Ahd because the **first thing it does is honour, not suspect**: the lender signs first, gives, and the counterparty receives a deed, not a demand — wrapped in the exact verse (2:282) that makes documenting a debt an act of *love and obedience*, not distrust. They use it because the reframe copy (§3.3) gives the borrower something no verbal IOU ever could: **the right to be finally, visibly settled — ذمّة محفوظة.** They return because the recurring-covenant hook (§3.4) turns a few-times-a-year act into a monthly ritual, and because each resolved obligation pays a dignity dividend. The bank becomes, quietly, *the witness to every Saudi's word* — a relationship no conventional bank can copy because only an Islamic bank can credibly stand on amana, wakala, and qard hassan.
