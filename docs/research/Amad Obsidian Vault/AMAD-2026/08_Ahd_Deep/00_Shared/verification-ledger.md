## How the verdicts reshaped the thesis (the honest synthesis)

- **The single biggest correction (C10):** the Musaned mandate does **not** auto-seed a two-sided interpersonal rail. We now claim only what is true — it guarantees mass wallet/KYC presence and lowers onboarding friction — and we **separate regulatory-driven adoption from organic k** explicitly.
- **The legal spine sharpened (C1, C5, C12, C14):** cite **ETL Art. 14** for signature equivalence, **ETL Art. 8** for record integrity (imported by **M/43 Art. 57**), **M/43 Arts. 55–59** for digital-evidence weight, and require an **accredited CSP/TSA** so the SEAL lands in Art. 57(1). Evidence is **presumptive/rebuttable**, and admissibility is a **judicial determination** (Arts. 4, 58, 61, 62).
- **The Shariah fee tightened (C2, C7, C13):** **actual DIRECT cost**, flat, decoupled from principal, on the **wakala/amana basis**, **board-approved** — and the two-contract device is the contestable part, so **free/float-monetised** is the conservatively safest default.
- **The payments lane corrected (C3):** in-flight funds are **Safeguarded Funds** with PSP-grade duties; the Sandbox is a **time-boxed test**, so production runs **as agent on the bank's own licensed rails.**
- **The algorithm corrected (C8, C15):** claim **≤ P−1 transfers** (each iteration zeroes ≥1 party), **not** "each party once" and **not** "minimum" (NP-hard).
