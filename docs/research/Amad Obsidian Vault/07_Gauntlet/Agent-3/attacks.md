# 🔪 Raw attacks I ran on my own 2 before sealing
> Every attack here was answered before the concept shipped. The sealed versions live in the Objection-Killer tables of [[concept-dayf]] / [[concept-waqar]]. If an attack below has no seal, the concept wouldn't have shipped.

## ضيف Dayf — attacks that nearly killed it
- **"It's just Nusuk Wallet again."** → reframed off payments onto **verification + protection + FX-fairness**; the hady-verified receipt is the wedge Nusuk has no answer to. *Sealed.*
- **"They leave — zero LTV, so no bank ships it."** → the hardest one. Reframed from an LTV product to a **funnel product** (seasonal FX/interchange volume + B2B2C + re-acquisition via Umrah/referral). Had this not closed, Dayf would have died here. *Sealed.*
- **"Verified sacrifice is vaporware."** → only survived because **Adahi is real + digitized in production**; if it weren't, I'd have cut the verification claim. *Sealed on a real rail.*
- **"Scam-shield = retired Haseen."** → demoted from a product to a **guest-protection feature** inside the companion; not a fraud-interceptor. *Sealed.*

## وقار Waqar — attacks that nearly killed it
- **"A child controlling a parent's account is a regulatory + ethical landmine."** → the decisive one. Redesigned to **consented, tiered, revocable supported-autonomy** (parent stays owner; help, not takeover). Without this distinction the concept is dead. *Sealed.*
- **"Worthy but not innovative — it's just big fonts + alerts."** → the novelty had to become the **'help without takeover' category + the dignity-guardian** (gentle check, not a block); if it stayed 'accessible UI,' it fails Innovation. *Sealed.*
- **"It's retired Haseen (fraud) / A2's Sawt (voice) in disguise."** → reframed as a **full elder-dignity platform**; protection + voice are pillars, not the product. *Sealed (added a dedicated table row).*
- **"Elders won't adopt a new app."** → solved by making the **adult child the installer** (motivated by birr al-walidayn); installer ≠ user. *Sealed.*

## The two attacks I could NOT fully seal (disclosed honestly)
- **Dayf** — *true* per-pilgrim retention is structurally low; I converted it to a funnel/brand play, but if a judge insists on per-user LTV, that's the soft seam (mitigated, not eliminated).
- **Waqar** — the **Data** vector is solid-not-spectacular (behavioral baseline is real but not ML-deep); honestly 16/20, the lowest-confidence claim.
