# Research: External Validation

## Decision: Target 150 eligible responses

**Rationale**: Current project already defines 150 as the threshold for replacing synthetic demand evidence.
Smaller samples may support direction, not population claims.  
**Alternatives considered**: 30-person convenience sample; wait for a representative national sample. Rejected
as too weak or too slow.

## Decision: Separate prevalence, strain, safeguards, and amount questions

**Rationale**: Findex addresses prevalence, not relationship harm or product fit. The project needs its own
measured evidence without implying national representativeness.  
**Alternatives considered**: One satisfaction question; reuse national figures. Rejected.

## Decision: Split reviewer questions

**Rationale**: Fee separation, pooling, multilateral settlement, mercy-first consent, and self-disclosure have
different bases and conditions. A compound approval would be ambiguous.  
**Alternatives considered**: One broad product approval. Rejected because it invites overclaiming.

## Decision: Grade external commitment

**Rationale**: Conversation, verbal interest, non-binding letter, pilot agreement, and paid contract represent
different evidence.  
**Alternatives considered**: Binary interested/not-interested. Rejected as misleading.
