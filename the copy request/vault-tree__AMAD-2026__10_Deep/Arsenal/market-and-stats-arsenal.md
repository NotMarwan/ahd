## 3. Market sizing — TAM / SAM / SOM

> **Method honesty:** the *inputs* below are sourced (🟢). The *sizing arithmetic* is a **MODEL** — a defensible top-down frame, not a measured market. We present it as "an order-of-magnitude frame anchored on cited inputs," never as a surveyed number. The single hardest input — **domestic interpersonal lending volume** — is 🔴 PENDING (see [[demand-evidence-ksa]] G5); we anchor TAM on the *defensible floor* instead.

### 3.1 TAM — the universe of "money between people" in KSA
- **Reachable people:** ~**35M Nafath-eligible residents** (nationals + Iqama holders) (M1/M2). *(Tourists without Iqama are scoped out — see [[legal-shariah-citations]] §3.)*
- **Floor on interpersonal money flow:** **SAR 213B/yr** in *cross-border* interpersonal remittances alone (M6) — before any *domestic* family lending, rent-splitting, or casual IOUs. Domestic interpersonal lending is **🔴 unmeasured** but structurally **larger** than the cross-border slice.
- **Documentation-demand signal:** **SAR 115.4B / 11 mo** of execution-court debt, **58.6% promissory notes** (M7) — the *disputed* tip of a much larger documented-and-undocumented base.
- **TAM framing (MODEL):** *"every adult resident is a potential party to multiple interpersonal-money events per year; the cross-border remittance floor alone is SAR 213B/yr, and the documented-debt enforcement load is SAR 100B+/yr — Ahd's addressable universe is the full interpersonal-money layer sitting on top of those floors."*

### 3.2 SAM — what the rails can actually serve today
- **Population reachable on the rails:** ~35M Nafath-eligible (M1) × **~74% banked** (M5) × **79% cashless behaviour** (M4) → the **tens of millions of residents already transacting digitally** with a gov-grade identity and an IBAN reachable by sarie (M10).
- **SAM = the Nafath-eligible, banked, digitally-active resident base** — the people who can create, sign, and settle an *ahd* with **zero new infrastructure**. This is the honest SAM: not "all 35M," but the banked-and-digital majority of them.

### 3.3 SOM — Alinma's realistic near-term wedge (MODEL)
Beachhead ladder (from [[00_MASTER_DOSSIER]] §4.3), each tied to a real channel:
1. **Salary / recurring covenants** — riding the mass wallet/KYC presence the Jan-2026 domestic-worker e-salary mandate guarantees *(presence, not a two-sided rail — see C10 honesty note, [[rebuttal-playbook]] Q-G3).*
2. **Flatmate rent / bills** — dense, Muqassa-native circles (local k>1).
3. **Family lending** — the emotional heart; high stakes, high pain.
4. **Group trips / weddings / casual promises** — the viral tail.
- **SOM = Alinma's existing customer base + the circles those customers pull in via the second-party requirement** (every ahd needs a counterparty → guest-Nafath acquisition). Sized off Alinma's own MAU once the wedge is chosen; **not claimed here as a hard number** (no Alinma-internal data — 🔴).

## 5. Muqassa & the netting/settlement scale

- **The feature:** Ahd's circle-netting is named after **مقاصّة (muqāṣṣa / set-off)** — a classical, valid Islamic-finance mechanism for offsetting mutual same-kind, same-currency debts (fiqh basis → [[legal-shariah-citations]] §6). **Do not confuse** it with *Muqassa* the Securities Clearing Center Company (a separate CMA entity) — Ahd's Muqassa is the **interpersonal-debt set-off feature**, not securities clearing.
- **Proven behaviour (🟢):** the prototype's greedy netting collapses **9 tangled IOUs → 2 transfers**, net-preserving, sum-zero; **O(E + n log n)**; bounded **≤ P−1 transfers** — *not* "the minimum" (NP-hard) and *not* "each party once" (verification C8/C15). Each leg executes only as a **consented novation**.
- **Settlement rail (🟢):** executes over **sarie** (M10) — instant, 24/7, ≤ SAR 20k/txn (installments > SAR 20k auto-split; no silent pull — credit-push only).
- **Scale framing:** the **SAR 213B remittance** + **SAR 115.4B enforcement** figures (M6/M7) are the order-of-magnitude of the money Ahd's record-and-settle layer sits on top of — netting is the wow that proves the data depth on top of that base.
