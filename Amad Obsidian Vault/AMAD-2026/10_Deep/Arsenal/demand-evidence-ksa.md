# 🎯 KSA Demand Evidence — killing "who asked for this?"

> **The wound (red-team A1, "most dangerous"):** every demand stat in the thesis was **US-sourced** (LendingTree / Bankrate). "Who asked for this?" was answered with an American survey and a Quranic verse — no Saudi voice, no Saudi number, no traction proxy. This file fixes that with **KSA-primary evidence**, states the **honest gaps**, and gives a **primary-data plan** to convert proxies into first-party proof before the pitch.
>
> **Confidence legend (used throughout the Arsenal):**
> 🟢 **SOLID** — named, linked, dated authoritative source directly supports the claim.
> 🟡 **PROXY** — closest defensible source (KSA-adjacent or non-KSA) standing in for the exact claim; labelled as such.
> 🔴 **PENDING** — no clean source found; primary data needed; flagged honestly so the red-team can't.

## 1. The one-line A1 kill

> **"Saudis don't need to be told to document money between people — they already do it at the scale of a national problem. Promissory notes (سندات لأمر) are the single largest category of debt hitting the Kingdom's execution courts — 58.6% of 571,251 enforcement requests worth SAR 115.4 billion in eleven months. The instrument exists, the habit exists, and it is so blunt and abuse-prone that SAMA had to ban finance firms from demanding it. What's missing is a fair, interest-free, structured version. That's Ahd."**

Source for the load-bearing number → **§2.1**.

## 2. The KSA-primary evidence (solid, sourced, dated)

### 2.1 🟢 SOLID — Promissory notes dominate Saudi debt enforcement
Saudi **execution courts (محاكم التنفيذ)** processed enforcement requests worth **SAR 115.4 billion across 571,251 requests in an 11-month period**, of which **58.6% (313,619 requests) were promissory notes (سندات لأمر)** — the **single largest category** of enforcement, ahead of every other instrument.
- Regional split (by value): Riyadh SAR 52.0B (121,611 reqs) · Makkah SAR 33.1B (155,311) · Eastern Province SAR 22.1B (93,643) · Madinah SAR 2.1B (30,326) · Qassim SAR 1.4B · Asir SAR 1.3B.
- **Source:** [Argaam — "محاكم التنفيذ تباشر طلبات بقيمة 115.4 مليار ريال في 11 شهراً.. 59% منها سندات لأمر"](https://www.argaam.com/ar/article/articledetail/id/1483475) — **published 24 Jul 2021** (data window FY2020–21).
- **Why it's load-bearing:** it is empirical, KSA-primary proof that the interpersonal-debt instrument is in **mass use** and that disputes over it are a **systemic load on the courts** — i.e., demand for a *fair, structured, dispute-preventing* version is not hypothetical.
- **Honesty caveat:** the figure is **2020–21 vintage** (re-source to the latest MoJ statistical yearbook pre-pitch if a fresher breakdown exists). It measures **enforcement traffic**, which is a strong **proxy** for the broader documentation habit, not a direct count of all interpersonal lending.

### 2.2 🟢 SOLID — The Kingdom runs on documented debt (Najiz scale)
The **Ministry of Justice delivered 43M+ e-services through Najiz in H1 2024**, with categories explicitly including **enforcement, documentation (توثيق), and acknowledgment (إقرار)** — the exact legal acts Ahd productizes.
- **Sources:** [Arab News, 17 Jul 2024](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2551501/saudi-arabia) · [SPA N2140615](https://www.spa.gov.sa/en/N2140615). Najiz now spans **160+ judicial services** across four portals (individuals/businesses/lawyers/government).

### 2.3 🟢 SOLID — E-promissory notes already adopted at scale (Nafith)
The MoJ's **نافذ / Nafith** e-promissory-note platform issued **800,000+ digital سند to 370,000+ beneficiaries** within roughly its first year, with direct electronic linkage to the execution courts.
- **Source:** [SPA — "وزارة العدل: 800 ألف سند رقمي أصدرتها نافذ لـ370 ألف مستفيد"](https://www.spa.gov.sa/w1579948) (≈2021).
- **Why it matters:** Saudis already accept a **fully digital, court-linked** promissory instrument. Ahd is not introducing a foreign behaviour; it is upgrading an instrument the market already trusts — but adding **qard-hasan structure, a payment schedule, settlement, and Shariah-clean terms** the bare سند lacks.
- **Precision (do not conflate):** **نافذ/Nafith** = the e-promissory-note issuance/registration platform; **ناجز/Najiz** = the broad MoJ services portal (incl. execution). The dossier sometimes used them interchangeably — keep them distinct in front of a Saudi reviewer.

### 2.4 🟢 SOLID — The blunt instrument is so abuse-prone the regulator intervened
**SAMA bars finance entities from requesting promissory notes (or other negotiable instruments) from individual consumers when offering credit-card financing products**, as a consumer-protection measure.
- **Source:** [SAMA Rulebook — "Not to Request Promissory Notes from Individual Consumers when Offering a Credit Card Financing Products"](https://rulebook.sama.gov.sa/en/not-request-promissory-notes-individual-consumers-when-offering-credit-card-financing-products); related [MoC promissory-note model rule](https://rulebook.sama.gov.sa/en/adhering-attaching-promissory-note-and-bill-exchange-models-prepared-ministry-commerce).
- **Scope honesty:** the ban is specifically about **credit-card financing products**, not a blanket prohibition on all promissory notes. State it precisely: *"the bare سند is abuse-prone enough that SAMA restricted finance firms from demanding it from consumers"* — which is exactly the gap a fair, structured, lender-signs-first instrument fills.

### 2.5 🟢 SOLID — The scale of money moving between people (remittance proxy)
Interpersonal **cross-border** money movement alone (a conservative proxy for "money between people"): **SAR 144.2B expatriate remittances + SAR 68.6B Saudi-national outward remittances ≈ SAR 213B in 2024** — before a single riyal of *domestic* family lending, rent-splitting, or casual IOUs.
- **Sources:** [Argaam — expat remittances SAR 144.2B in 2024 (SAMA)](https://www.argaam.com/en/article/articledetail/id/1788759) · [Saudi Gazette — expat remittances +14% in 2024](https://saudigazette.com.sa/article/649194); Saudi-national outward figure SAR 68.6B (+11% YoY) per the same SAMA series.

### 2.6 🟢 SOLID — A large irregular-cash-flow population that lends and borrows
**2.25M+ freelancers / self-employed** were registered in KSA by **Sept 2024** (sector ≈ **SAR 72.5B / 2% of GDP in 2023**) — a population with lumpy income, frequent interpersonal float, and a documented appetite for formal proof.
- **Sources:** [Global Business Outlook — "over two million freelancers"](https://globalbusinessoutlook.com/economy/over-two-million-freelancers-saudi-arabia-join-national-economy/) · [MHRSD freelance-document service](https://www.hrsd.gov.sa/en/ministry-services/services/1065686).

### 2.7 🟢 SOLID — The competitive whitespace is real (Splitwise gap)
The incumbent behaviour-tracker, **Splitwise, has no Arabic** (a documented complaint from KSA users), only *counts* debts (no settlement, no witness, no enforceable record), and its tightened free tier is pushing Saudi users to alternatives.
- **Sources:** [Splitwise on the Saudi App Store](https://apps.apple.com/sa/app/splitwise/id458023433) · [Spliteroo — Splitwise alternatives in Saudi Arabia](https://www.spliteroo.app/blog/6-best-splitwise-alternatives-for-managing-group-expenses-in-saudi-arabia).

## 4. The US proxies — cite them honestly, never as KSA fact

These are **US surveys**. Use them only to illustrate that the *relational* pain is a universal human pattern, then pivot immediately to the KSA-primary scale (§2).
- 🟡 **31%** of US adults say a friend or family member owes them money. — [LendingTree, friend/family owes-money survey](https://www.lendingtree.com/personal/friend-or-family-owes-money-survey/)
- 🟡 **~1 in 6** say money has **ruined a relationship**; top reason (41%) = a loan not repaid; another 27% report tension. — [LendingTree, lending-between-family-friends study](https://www.lendingtree.com/personal/study-lending-between-family-friends/)
- 🟡 **70%** of US adults have lent money / fronted a group expense expecting repayment; **55%** had ≥1 negative consequence; **26%** a damaged relationship. — [Bankrate lending-money survey](https://www.bankrate.com/credit-cards/news/lending-money-survey/) · [Bankrate 2019](https://www.bankrate.com/credit-cards/news/lending-money-survey-2019/)
- **Label in all materials:** *"US surveys — illustrative of a universal pattern; KSA primary figure pending (see §5)."*

## 5. Primary-data plan (turn 🔴/🟡 into 🟢 before the pitch)

**Goal:** one shard of *real Saudi voice* beats every US statistic. Three options, ranked by effort/payoff, all completable in the enrichment window.

### 5.1 Fast, free, defensible — review & artefact mining (1 day)
- **App-store + social mining:** harvest Arabic reviews/complaints on Splitwise & expense-split apps, and X/TikTok threads on "أقرضت ومارجعوا لي الفلوس" / "سند لأمر بين الأصحاب" → 8–12 verbatim Saudi quotes. Output: a quote wall ("في صوتهم", in their own words).
- **Court-data refresh:** pull the **latest MoJ statistical yearbook / Najiz dashboard** to refresh §2.1's promissory-note share to 2023–24 and close G4.

### 5.2 Best evidence-per-hour — 15–20 structured interviews (2–3 days)
- **Segments:** flatmates (rent split), a parent who lent an adult child, a freelancer who floats clients, a trip-circle organizer.
- **Instrument (5 questions, do not lead):** (1) Last time you lent/borrowed from someone you know — what happened? (2) Did you write anything down? Why / why not? (3) Has money ever strained a relationship? (4) Would a *plain, interest-free, both-sign* record have helped — or felt cold? (5) Who would you trust to hold it?
- **Output:** % who didn't document, % who felt strain, the exact phrases that signal demand → a KSA-primary replacement for G1/G2.

### 5.3 Quantified, citable — a micro-survey (3–5 days, n≥150)
- One-screen Arabic survey via X / WhatsApp groups / university lists. Measures: documentation rate, strain incidence, willingness-to-use, trust-in-bank-as-scribe. Even n=150 gives a *Saudi* number to put on the slide.

> **Deliverable for the deck:** a single demand slide — **KSA-primary court/Najiz scale (§2)** on top, **one first-party shard (§5.2 quote or §5.3 stat)** as the human proof, US %s relegated to a labelled footnote. That sequencing is what makes A1 un-attackable.
