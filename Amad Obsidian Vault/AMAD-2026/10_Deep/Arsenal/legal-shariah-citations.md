## 1. The admissibility stack (the keystone)

Ahd manufactures **near-incontestable proof at the moment of consent**. The claim ceiling is always *"designed to meet the conditions of admissibility"* — never *"is admissible"* (the court admits; we satisfy the conditions).

### 1.1 🟢 Law of Evidence 2022 — Royal Decree **M/43**
- **Citation:** Royal Decree **No. M/43**, dated **26/05/1443H (≈ 31 Dec 2021)**; **in force 7 July 2022.** Digital records, digital documents, and electronic signatures are recognised as **documentary evidence** when integrity/attribution conditions are met.
- **The articles that matter (Part 4, "Digital/Electronic Evidence", Arts. ~53–64):**
  - Digital evidence given **writing-equivalent force**; unofficial digital evidence tied to the Electronic Transactions Law (**Art. 57**).
  - **Burden shifts to the party contesting** the digital evidence (**Art. 58**) — *"reliable unless proven otherwise."*
  - **Judicial determination** of weight, including on the court's own motion (**Arts. 4, 61, 62**); submission in original/other digital form (**Art. 60**).
- **Sources:** [Al Tamimi — "The Saudi New Law of Evidence"](https://www.tamimi.com/law-update-articles/the-saudi-new-law-of-evidence-new-page-in-the-saudi-judiciary-history/) · [Al Tamimi — characteristics of electronic & digital evidence](https://www.tamimi.com/law-update-articles/the-characteristics-electronic-and-digital-evidences-in-saudi-arabia/) · [QHM Law Firm — digital evidence](https://qhmlawfirm.com/wp/digital-evidence-law/) · [Ghazzawi Law Firm — major provisions](https://www.ghazzawilawfirm.com/insights/major-provisions-and-changes-in-saudi-arabias-law-of-evidence/) · [EastLaws — RD No. 43/2022 full text](https://www.eastlaws.com/legislation-full-text/en/saudi-arabia/royal-decree/07-01-2022/no-43).
- **Residual ambiguity (🔴):** exact **publication date drifts** across sources — *issued 30/31 Dec 2021, published 1 Jan or 7 Jan 2022, in force 7 Jul 2022.* The **substance** (digital evidence recognised; burden on challenger) is firm; **counsel should confirm the exact article numbers and date** in a pre-production opinion. Evidence is **presumptive/rebuttable**, not an irrebuttable instrument — state it that way.

### 1.2 🟢 Electronic Transactions Law 2007 — Royal Decree **M/18** (resolve the M/8 vs M/18 ambiguity)
- **THE RESOLUTION:** The official Bureau of Experts at the Council of Ministers publishes the ETL as **Royal Decree No. M/18, dated 8/3/1428H (27 March 2007).** "**M/8**" appears in some secondary sources (older MCIT / WIPO Lex references) and is a **citation variant**, not a second law. **→ Cite M/18, per laws.boe.gov.sa; flag M/8 as the variant counsel will reconcile.**
- **Source for M/18:** [Bureau of Experts (laws.boe.gov.sa) — ETL Royal Decree No. M/18, 27 March 2007 (official PDF)](https://laws.boe.gov.sa/Files/Download/?attId=758143f9-52ec-4a6f-92c8-adbb011cf516).
- **The articles that matter:**
  - **Art. 14 (esp. 14-1): an electronic signature has the SAME legal effect as a handwritten signature** when reliability conditions are met. *(Correction folded in: equivalence is **Art. 14**, NOT Art. 8 — verification C1/C5.)* — confirmed by the licensed TSP itself: [emdha — "digitally-signed documents under Article 14-1 of the e-Transactions Law"](https://www.emdha.sa/).
  - **Art. 8: record originality / integrity test** (the record is unchanged since finalisation) — the article M/43 Art. 57 *imports*.
- **Commentary sources:** [Lexology — enforceability & admissibility of e-signatures in KSA](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=f3cf0b56-c21c-4126-a351-e927de9b5649) · [Derayah / saudilegal — Electronic Transactions](https://www.saudilegal.com/saudi-law-overview/electronic-transactions) · [eSignGlobal — KSA ETL](https://www.esignglobal.com/blog/saudi-arabia-electronic-transactions-law).
- **Exclusions to flag (🟢):** e-signatures are **not** valid for certain acts (wills, some real-estate, personal-status matters) — irrelevant to an interpersonal qard, but name it so the boundary is explicit.

### 1.3 🟢 The e-signature / TSP basis (Nafath ≠ the signature)
- **The two-layer truth:** **Nafath = identity assurance / authentication** (gov-grade). The **legally-weighted signature** is an **Advanced (AES) / Qualified (QES) electronic signature** issued by a **CST-licensed, DGA-regulated Trust Service Provider** that *consumes* Nafath identity.
- **The TSP:** **emdha** — the **first Saudi company licensed by CST (Communications, Space & Technology Commission), regulated by DGA (Digital Government Authority), WebTrust-certified**, operating under the **Saudi National Root CA** (owned by Baud Telecom Co.). Offers digital signature + **RFC-3161 timestamp** services. *(emdha is one licensed TSP — keep "e.g."; it is not the only path.)*
- **Sources:** [emdha.sa — about](https://www.emdha.sa/about-us) · [Cloud Signature Consortium — emdha TSP](https://cloudsignatureconsortium.org/member/emdha-trust-service-provider-tsp/).
- **The honest binding caveat (🟡→🔴):** **Nafath alone yields authentication → a basic/standard e-signature → presumptive evidence.** Full **QES-grade weight** requires the **licensed-CSP certificate layered on top** (verification C9/C14). For non-customers signing by Nafath alone, claim *"Nafath-auth + presumptive evidence,"* and add the CSP layer for binding strength.

### 1.4 🔴 The one genuinely-open permission (name it before they do)
Whether **SDAIA/Nafath + a TSP permit Nafath-AES for *interpersonal private debt*** (vs the gov/enterprise use-cases they're provisioned for) is **assumed, not confirmed** (red-team A9). → A **pre-production validation item**, not a redesign. Posture: *"designed to meet the conditions; the use-case permission is a named validation step."*

## 3. The Shariah fee construction (the most contested element — handle precisely)

### 3.1 🟢 AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 19 (Loan / Qard) — the exact rule
- **Service charge is permitted only at ACTUAL (direct) cost.** Any amount **in excess of the actual cost is prohibited** (riba). — [AAOIFI SS-19](https://aaoifi.com/ss-19-loan-qard/?lang=en) · [State Bank of Pakistan — Compendium of AAOIFI Shariah Standards](https://www.sbp.org.pk/ifpd/compendium/Compendium.pdf).
- **The charge may NOT be linked to the amount lent** — **SS-19 clause 10/3/2** ("it is not permitted to link the charge to the amount withdrawn/given"). A %-of-principal fee is therefore **riba**. — [EUDL comparative study (Hidayat et al., 2022)](https://eudl.eu/pdf/10.4108/eai.4-11-2022.2329681).
- **"Actual cost" excludes indirect costs** (salaries, office rent, general overhead) — only **direct** servicing cost. — same EUDL / SBP sources.
- **Bundling test (the contestable part):** **SS-19 cl. 7/8** bars combining a commutative contract **as a condition of** the qard, invoking the **Hilah (stratagem) doctrine.** The separate wakala/documentation service must be **genuine and independent**, never a precondition of the loan.

### 3.2 The defensible posture (folded from verification C2/C7/C13)
1. **Qard = zero fee, zero markup, zero penalty.** The bank is not a party to it.
2. Any fee lives on a **separate wakala/amana service contract** — **flat, decoupled from amount AND tenor, actual DIRECT cost only.**
3. **The two-contract device is the single most contestable element** (the Hilah test) → the **conservatively safest default is FREE at the consumer layer, float-monetised** (sidesteps the fee-riba question entirely).
4. **The figure, the methodology, and the separation all require Alinma's own Shariah board** — state this **conditionally**: *"IF a flat fee benchmarked to documented actual servicing cost is charged on a wakala basis, THEN it is defensible — and the board signs the figure."*
- **No Alinma-specific product doc exists (🔴)** — never assert Alinma *already* prices this way; frame it as a clean *design*.

## 5. PDPL (data protection)

- **🟢 Personal Data Protection Law:** in force **14 Sep 2023**; **fully enforceable from 14 Sep 2024** (after a one-year grace period); regulator = **SDAIA**. — [Morgan Lewis](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2024/09/saudi-arabia-personal-data-protection-law-transition-period-ends-september-14) · [Lexology — new SDAIA rules now enforceable](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=a42ff6b6-8427-4c24-a33d-8a6f4a5271e4).
- **Ahd's PDPL design:** dual lawful basis (**explicit consent + contract necessity**); **minimization** (store a verified-identity *assertion* + national-ID **hash**, never raw biometrics/ID images); **in-Kingdom residency**; **evidentiary-hold exemption** resolves the retention-vs-erasure conflict (disclosed at signing). *(Detail in [[layer-legal-shariah-regulatory]] §3.8.)*

## 6. Dispute / enforcement boundary (the honest ceiling)

- Ahd's role **ends at the evidence bundle.** It is **strong documentary evidence, NOT itself an enforcement instrument (سند تنفيذي).**
- Path: in-app respite (2:280) → **evidence bundle** (signed PDF/A of the iqrar + SHA-256 + timestamp + Nafath refs + audit log) → user files on **Taradhi (تراضي)** for reconciliation → failing that, a competent court → judgment → **Najiz** execution.
- The iqrar makes any later adjudication **short and one-sided** — that's the claim, no more. *(Najiz/Taradhi structure: [[layer-legal-shariah-regulatory]] §3.7.)*
