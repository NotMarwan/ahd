# 🎴 Wildcard (cross-lane) — نَذر · Nadhr — "Keep the promise you made to God."

> Out of my Invisible-Saudis segment lane on purpose — this is a **New Primitive × Faith-Positive** leap, parked for portfolio range.

**One-liner:** *A bank primitive that lets you make and faithfully honor a **nadhr** — a charitable vow ("if my daughter recovers, I will give SAR X to orphans") — by escrowing the commitment so it auto-fulfils to a verified cause when the moment comes, instead of being forgotten.*

## The leap
Millions of Muslims make a **nadhr** (a vow to give if a duʿāʾ is answered, or in gratitude) — it is one of the most emotionally charged financial acts in the faith, made at moments of fear, hope, and thanks. And it is **completely unmanaged**: people forget, lose track of how much, or never fulfil a vow that is religiously **binding**. No bank has ever helped a Muslim *keep a promise to God.* That is an "I've never seen this" idea with deep emotional truth.

## How it works
Make a nadhr in the app (amount/act + the intention + a beneficiary type). Dhimmah-style, the bank **ring-fences** the committed amount (or auto-accrues it), reminds you, and — when you mark the condition fulfilled, or on a date — **executes the giving** to a verified Shariah-clean channel, with a record so the vow is provably honored. A gentle ledger of "vows kept."

## Why it's a wildcard, not the champion
- **Strengths:** uniquely Islamic, profoundly emotional, a genuinely *new* financial primitive (a self-honoring charitable commitment), Shariah-clean (it *fulfils* an obligation — no riba, and **no chance/gharar**: you give regardless; the "condition" is your own vow, not a wager), only an Islamic bank could host it credibly.
- **Why not champion:** it sits in the **Faith-Positive / New-Primitive** vectors (Agents 3/4's territory), not my Invisible-Saudis segment lane; and fiqh of nadhr has nuance (binding vs. recommended vows) that needs a Shariah board to scope cleanly. A strong portfolio diversifier and a natural **module of [[concept-dhimmah|Dhimmah]]** (clearing obligations to God alongside obligations to people).

## Shariah note
Honoring a nadhr is fulfilling a binding obligation, not gambling — frame strictly as "help me keep a vow I already made," never "pledge X if Y happens" as a wager. Board-scoped; AI never rules on whether a given vow is binding.
