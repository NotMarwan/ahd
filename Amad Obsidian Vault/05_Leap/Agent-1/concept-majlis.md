# 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 مجلس Majlis — the family's shared *wakil*
**Track:** 1 · GenAI · **Requirements:** /02 AI + /03 CX + /04 Sustainability · **Score: 82/100**
*Different bet from [[concept-wakeel|Wakeel]]: collective, multi-principal agency — the family, not the individual.*

## A. One-liner
**"Majlis is a shared digital wakil for a Saudi family — parents, adult children and elders each delegate a slice of authority, and one trusted agent autonomously runs the family's money life: the household bills, the kids' allowances, the support that flows up to grandparents and down to students — every member seeing only what they should."**

## B. Problem (human truth)
Saudi finance is **familial**, not individual — money flows across three generations (children support aging parents; parents fund students; siblings split an elder's care). Today this is a mess of manual transfers, awkward reminders, and forgotten shares — and **no bank product is built for the family as the unit.** The emotional truth: nobody wants to be the sibling who "didn't pay their share" of mom's care, or the son who forgot.

## C. Solution
A **multi-principal wakala**: each family member grants the shared agent a scoped mandate (contribute SAR X to the elder-care pool; receive a student stipend; approve household bills). Majlis then **autonomously coordinates + executes** (PIS): collects each sibling's share for a parent's care, runs the household standing dues, releases kids' allowances on rules, and keeps a **transparent family ledger** with privacy walls (the kids don't see the elder-care amounts). Disputes/edge cases escalate to the family "head" or a vote.
**Only-now:** PIS execution + ALLaM Arabic + wakala (which natively supports *multiple principals delegating to one agent*).

## D. Mapping
Track 1; /02 AI + /03 CX + /04 Sustainability (multi-generational financial resilience). Single track ✓.

## E. 72h build plan
Shared-mandate model + role-based views + the coordination/execution engine on synthetic family data (3 roles: working son, student daughter, elder parent). *Built:* multi-principal scope + the elder-care collection + allowance release + family ledger. *Mocked:* PIS rails, ALLaM (seam). **Must-work:** three siblings' shares auto-collected into an elder-care payment, executed + shown in the family ledger with privacy walls intact.

## F. Data story
Cross-member contribution + obligation coordination: who owes what to the shared pools, shortfall detection, fair-share rebalancing — shown as a live family money-flow the agent *acts on*.

## G. Demo script (3 min)
A family sets up Majlis → three siblings each delegate SAR 700/mo to "mom's care" + a student stipend rule → fast-forward: the agent **auto-collects the three shares, pays the elder-care bill, releases the student's stipend**, and shows each member their own privacy-scoped ledger. Close: "Saudi money is a family, not an individual. This is the first bank product that treats it that way — and only a wakala can let many people trust one agent."

## H. Alinma-ship case
Captures the **whole family graph** (multi-account stickiness across generations = deposit gravity + lifetime value); on-strategy for a family-centric Shariah bank; Shariah-clean (multi-principal wakala; no lending). PDPL via per-member consent/scope; SAMA PIS rails.

## I. Differentiation
- vs [[concept-wakeel|Wakeel]]: individual vs **family** agency (composes — Majlis is Wakeel at family scale).
- vs splitting apps (Western): they *track* who owes; Majlis *collects + executes + coordinates* across generations under one trusted agent, with Islamic family duties (nafaqah/birr) as first-class.
- Not on kill-list (no credit/score/game).

## J. Risk register
1. Multi-principal consent + privacy is the hard part → *mitigation:* role-based scopes + a clear "family head" arbiter; demo one clean flow.
2. Family-politics edge cases → *mitigation:* escalation/vote, not silent action.
3. Demo complexity → keep to 3 roles, one elder-care flow.
